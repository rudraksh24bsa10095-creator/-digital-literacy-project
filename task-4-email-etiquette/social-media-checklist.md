#	Do
1	Think before you post — Consider whether the content reflects your values and would be appropriate for professors, employers, or family members to see.
2	Use privacy settings — Regularly review and update your privacy controls to manage who can see your posts, stories, and personal information.
3	Verify information before sharing — Fact-check news and claims through reliable sources to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.
4	Maintain a professional presence — Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase academic achievements, projects, and career interests in a polished manner.
5	Take digital breaks — Set boundaries for social media usage to prioritize academics, mental health, and face-to-face interactions.

#	Don't
1	Don't post in anger or distress — Avoid venting frustrations online, as impulsive posts can damage reputations and relationships permanently.
2	Don't share confidential information — Never post your location patterns, ID cards, financial details, or sensitive academic/work-related content.
3	Don't engage with trolls or bullies — Refrain from responding to inflammatory comments; block, report, or disengage instead.
4	Don't overshare personal struggles — Be cautious about posting mental health crises, relationship conflicts, or family matters in public forums.
5	Don't post during class or work hours — Avoid social media distractions in professional and academic settings to demonstrate respect and focus.
